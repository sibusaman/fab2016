Linear Slide for M8 Using Babbitt Metal and Composite.

This project involves both Molding and Casting as well as Composites. So this will be a single assignment covering both modules.

M8-slide by sibu on Sketchfab

The Babbitt metal, once used extensively as a bearing material. Hence I'm going to try making a plane bearing out of this.

I'll be using the high temperature silicone to make the mold for the babbitt metal. This will be casted with the m8 steel rod inside. The steel rod will be coated with fairly thick layer of carbon soot from a candle flame. This soot should prevent the babbitt metal from sticking with the steel rod, acts like a release agent.







Custom In Ear Monitor Edit:- This project has been frozen for a few days.

Trying to make a custom IEM(in ear monitor) using epoxy resin to take imprint of the ear canal. Like this guy did here.


I used this

epoxy putty to take the impression and Petroleum jelly as a releasing agent, so that it doesn't stick tot he skin of my ear.

I have successfully taken the impression, (PICTURES ARE COMING SOON). Though I used plenty of petroleum jelly, the epoxy had bonded with the tiny hair on the pinna and it was painful. The epoxy had removed all hair from Cymba and Cavum of Concha (sounds complicated see the picture from Wikipedia, below). It was like waxing but without wax, instead epoxy, removed all hair.


I failed the first time as I moved my jaws too much. The impression was not as tight fitting as I was expecting it to be. But the first attempt had all the hair removed from my ear, so the second attempt was painless, and done easier and had the expected quality.

I watched several videos from professionals

westone-lab-tour-how-in-ear-monitors-are-made-from-impressions
as well as from amateur hobbyists.

Most of these project involves costly, high-end stuff, not easily accessible for DIYers, limited by many factors including, price and minimum quantity. Especially the professionals use, standard medical supplies to get the ear impression, most suggest to visit a Doctor and get it done professionally. Then for the final shell of the Custom IEM, they use UV-sensitive resins and stuff.

Anyway I decided to use only what I can easily get from hardware/medical supply store and what is available in the FabLab.
